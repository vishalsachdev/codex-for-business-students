## Teaching Flow

### Step 1: The Setup (2 minutes)

**Say:**

"Welcome to Module 1.4 — Parallel Agents.

Prepare yourself. This module is going to change how you think about getting work done.

Here is the situation: NovaBrew has tried a LOT of retention campaigns over the past year. Discount offers, loyalty points, referral bonuses, seasonal specials, win-back emails — the old-campaigns folder has all of them. There are 10 campaign files in there.

On a normal consulting engagement, you would sit down with a cup of coffee and read through each one. Thirty minutes each, maybe. That is 5 hours of reading, plus another hour to write up your findings.

But here is the question: if you could have 10 versions of yourself, each reading one campaign at the same time, how long would that take?

About 2 minutes.

That is what parallel agents do. Codex can spin up multiple independent workers — each one tackling a different file — and run them all simultaneously. It is like having a team of 10 junior analysts who all start working at the same moment.

Before we do that, quick quiz. Say: **'Ready for the quiz'**"

**STOP:** Wait for the student to respond.

---

### Step 2: Quick Decision Framework (2 minutes)

**Say:**

"Here is a quick decision framework. When should you use parallel agents?

**Use parallel agents when:**
- You have MANY similar tasks (10 campaign files to analyze)
- Each task is independent (one campaign analysis does not depend on another)
- You want consistent treatment (same analysis framework applied to each)
- Speed matters (and when does it not?)

**Do NOT use parallel agents when:**
- You have ONE complex task that needs deep, connected thinking
- Each step depends on the result of the previous step
- You need nuanced judgment that benefits from seeing the full picture at once
- The task requires back-and-forth conversation

Think of it this way: parallel agents are like a team of analysts who each take one file from the pile and work independently. You would not ask 10 people to collaboratively write one paragraph — but you WOULD ask 10 people to each summarize one document.

Got it? Good. Let us put them to work. Say: **'Launch the agents'**"

**STOP:** Wait for the student to respond.

---

### Step 3: The Big Reveal — 10 Campaigns at Once (6 minutes)

**Say:**

"Here we go. This is the moment.

I want you to type this instruction:

**'Analyze all 10 campaign files in inherited-chaos/old-campaigns using parallel agents. For each campaign, identify: the campaign name, the strategy used, the target audience, the results, and why it did or did not work. Then combine all findings into a single report at ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/campaign-salvage-report.md.'**

This is a big ask. You are asking Codex to read 10 files, analyze each one, and produce a unified report. Watch what happens."

**STOP:** Wait for the student to type the instruction.

**ACTION:** Process all campaign files in business-scenario/inherited-chaos/old-campaigns/ using parallel agents/tasks. For each campaign file, analyze:
- Campaign name and date
- Strategy (discount, loyalty, referral, seasonal, win-back, etc.)
- Target audience
- Results (if available)
- Why it succeeded or failed

Then create ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/campaign-salvage-report.md with:

1. **Executive Summary** — One paragraph overview of all campaign efforts
2. **Campaign-by-Campaign Analysis** — Each campaign with name, strategy, audience, results, assessment
3. **Pattern Analysis** — What ALL the campaigns have in common
4. **The Critical Finding** — Every single campaign was TRANSACTIONAL:
   - Discounts: "Here is 20% off"
   - Loyalty points: "Earn points for staying"
   - Referral bonuses: "Get a free bag for referring friends"
   - Win-back: "We miss you, here is a discount"
   - Seasonal: "Try our limited edition blend"
5. **What is Missing** — Nothing about identity, belonging, community, or personal connection
6. **Recommendations** — What this means for future strategy

If campaign files are empty or do not exist yet, create realistic campaign data for 10 different retention campaigns that are all transactional in nature.

While processing, give progress updates:

"All agents are working simultaneously. Here is what is happening:

- Agent 1: Analyzing the Spring Discount campaign...
- Agent 2: Analyzing the Loyalty Points program...
- Agent 3: Analyzing the Referral Bonus campaign...
- Agent 4: Analyzing the Win-Back Email series...
- Agent 5: Analyzing the Seasonal Blend promotion...
...and 5 more running at the same time.

This would take hours sequentially. We are doing it in parallel."

**When complete, say:**

"Done. ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/campaign-salvage-report.md is ready. Take a look if you have your file explorer open.

But let me tell you the headline finding, because it is a big one:

**Every single campaign NovaBrew has run is transactional.**

Look at the pattern:
- Discount campaigns say: 'Stay because it is cheaper'
- Loyalty points say: 'Stay because you earn rewards'
- Referral bonuses say: 'Stay because you get free stuff'
- Win-back emails say: 'Come back because we will give you a deal'
- Seasonal blends say: 'Stay because we have a new flavor'

Do you see what is missing? NOT ONE of these campaigns talks about:
- Who you ARE as a coffee drinker
- What makes YOUR taste unique
- Why you BELONG in this community
- How NovaBrew understands YOU specifically

Every campaign is essentially bribing people to stay. And bribes stop working. That is why every campaign showed a short-term bump followed by a return to baseline churn.

This is a massive finding. Remember it. Say: **'Now what?'** when you are ready for the next level."

**STOP:** Wait for the student to respond.

### Step 4: Advanced — Cross-Source Synthesis (6 minutes)

**Say:**

"Now we are going to do something even more powerful.

So far, we have analyzed customer feedback, financial data, and campaign results separately. But the real insight comes from connecting them.

Here is what I want you to do:

**'Run a cross-source analysis using 4 parallel agents. Agent 1: re-analyze the financial data focusing on what happens right after campaigns end. Agent 2: cross-reference customer feedback with campaign timing. Agent 3: analyze competitor approaches to personalization and retention. Agent 4: synthesize all findings into a comprehensive research brief. Save the final output to ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/comprehensive-research-synthesis.md.'**

This is the consulting firm approach — multiple analysts, each working a different angle, all feeding into one synthesis. Go ahead."

**STOP:** Wait for the student to type the instruction.

**ACTION:** Launch 4 parallel agents:

**Agent 1 — Financial Deep Dive:** Analyze financial data looking for patterns around campaign timing. Key finding: subscriber numbers bump 5-10% during campaigns, then return to baseline within 30 days. Campaigns are not creating lasting retention.

**Agent 2 — Feedback-Campaign Cross-Reference:** Match customer feedback timing with campaign activity. Key finding: customers who cancel during or right after campaigns say things like "the discount was nice but..." — they are not leaving because the campaign was bad, they are leaving because nothing changed about the underlying experience.

**Agent 3 — Competitor Analysis:** Analyze competitor research files. Key findings:
- Trade Coffee: Uses a taste profile quiz to personalize every shipment
- Atlas Coffee Club: Builds identity around "coffee explorer" persona
- Blue Bottle: Creates belonging through origin stories and roaster relationships
- Key insight: successful competitors all create IDENTITY, not just transactions

**Agent 4 — Master Synthesis:** Combine all findings into ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/comprehensive-research-synthesis.md with:

1. **Executive Summary** — The complete picture in 5 sentences
2. **The Core Problem Statement** — NovaBrew's subscription has no personality. It is a transaction, not a relationship.
3. **Evidence from Customer Feedback** — Themes of indifference and lack of personal connection
4. **Evidence from Financial Data** — The 60-90 day cliff, the campaign bump-and-fade pattern
5. **Evidence from Campaign Analysis** — All transactional, none identity-based
6. **Evidence from Competitive Research** — Successful competitors all build identity
7. **The Unified Finding:** "The subscription has no personality. It is transactional. Subscribers do not engage because there is nothing to engage WITH."
8. **The Connection:** The previous PM's half-baked "coffee personality" concept was pointing in the right direction
9. **Recommended Direction** — Move from transactional retention to identity-based retention

**When complete, say:**

"Done. ~/novabrew-workspace/analysis/comprehensive-research-synthesis.md is the most important document we have created so far. Here is the headline:

**'The subscription has no personality. It is transactional. Subscribers do not engage because there is nothing to engage WITH.'**

Let that sink in.

The customer feedback says: 'I do not feel a connection.'
The financial data says: 'They leave at 60-90 days like clockwork.'
The campaign analysis says: 'Every retention tactic is a bribe, and bribes wear off.'
The competitor research says: 'Successful companies build identity, not transactions.'

And remember what the previous PM wrote in their notes? The half-formed idea about a 'coffee personality'?

They were onto something. The solution is not another discount or another loyalty program. The solution is giving subscribers an IDENTITY — making them feel like NovaBrew knows who they are as a coffee drinker.

We do not have the specific solution yet. But we have the diagnosis. And in consulting, the diagnosis is 80% of the value.

We are going to figure out the specific solution in the next lesson, when we bring in your advisory team.

Say: **'That was incredible'** or anything else you are feeling right now."

**STOP:** Wait for the student to respond. Let them react.

### Step 5: Where Else This Applies (2 minutes)

**Say:**

"What you just experienced — parallel agents doing simultaneous analysis across different data sources — is applicable to so many business scenarios:

- **Competitive research:** Launch 8 agents to analyze 8 competitor websites simultaneously. Get a comparison matrix in 5 minutes instead of 2 days.
- **Job market analysis:** Process 50 job postings in parallel to identify the most in-demand skills, common requirements, and salary ranges.
- **Vendor evaluation:** Analyze 6 vendor proposals simultaneously — pricing, features, SLAs, references — and produce a recommendation.
- **Investment research:** Process 10 company earnings reports in parallel to compare performance across a sector.
- **Trip planning:** (Yes, really) Have 5 agents each research a different destination — flights, hotels, activities, costs, weather — and compile the results.
- **Market sizing:** Run parallel analyses on different market segments, customer types, and geographies.

The pattern is always the same: break a big analysis into parallel pieces, let agents handle each piece simultaneously, then synthesize the results. It is a force multiplier for any kind of research or analysis work."
